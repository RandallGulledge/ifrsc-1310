Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Progressive Enhancement is a good source to use for any website and it has a lot of good content, presentation, and client-side scripting. Just as the article compared Progressive Enhancement to a Peanut M&M it shows how it really works. The content is solid, the presentation is rich, and the client-side scripting tops it off. Overall it is a great source to use but it does have its limits and should be improved to give more emphasis on CSS and JavaScript than HTML. I think the content is great and does a good job of how much work was put into it. CSS and JavaScript are good too and have good taste in their designs but only use a small part of it to make them better.
